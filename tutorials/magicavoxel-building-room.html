Building your first room

MagicaVoxel tutorial · Part 2 · Beginner

In Part 1 we placed our first voxels and set up a canvas. Now let's combine voxels into a small room: a floor, two walls, and a simple piece of furniture.

1. Plan your layout

Before placing voxels, decide roughly how big your room will be within your canvas. For a 24 x 24 x 24 canvas, a good starting point is:

  • Floor: a flat square covering most of the base, e.g. 20 x 20 voxels, 1 voxel thick.
  • Walls: along two adjacent edges of the floor, a few voxels tall (e.g. 8 voxels).
  • Furniture: small shapes placed away from the walls.

2. Build the floor

  1. Select the Voxel tool and pick a base color (e.g. a warm wood tone).
  2. Use the Box tool (often labeled "B" or found in the tool palette) to draw a filled rectangle in one click-drag instead of placing voxels one by one.
  3. Draw a flat rectangle on the bottom layer of your canvas to form the floor.
Tip: hold the modifier shown in the tool tooltip (often Shift) while using the Box tool to constrain it to a single layer — handy for keeping the floor perfectly flat.

3. Build the walls

  1. Pick a different color for the walls so they read clearly against the floor.
  2. Switch to a side view to make it easier to build straight up.
  3. Using the Box tool, draw a tall, thin rectangle along one edge of the floor, several voxels high — this is your first wall.
  4. Repeat along an adjacent edge of the floor for the second wall.

4. Add furniture

Furniture in voxel art is just smaller groups of voxels in different colors. Try building:

  • A small bed or sofa: a low, wide block with a slightly taller block as a backrest.
  • A table: four thin voxel "legs" with a flat slab on top.
  • A shelf: a thin vertical block against a wall with small colored voxels on top as "books" or "items".

Place these on top of the floor, leaving some empty space so the room doesn't feel cramped.

5. Check your isometric view

Rotate the view (right-click drag) until you're looking at the room from a corner — this is the classic isometric room composition, showing two walls and the floor at once.

MagicaVoxel includes a built-in renderer (the Render tab). Even at this early stage, try a quick render to get a feel for how lighting affects your colors.
